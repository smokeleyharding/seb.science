About

My research examines how law, ethics, and emerging technologies interact, with a focus on artificial intelligence in healthcare and the human right to benefit from scientific progress. I develop governance frameworks for responsible technology use that aim to accelerate, rather than impede, scientific and medical progress.

My work has appeared in Nature Machine Intelligence, NEJM AI, and Cambridge University Press, and has been covered in Nature and Science. I hold doctorates in Law (Oxford) and Psychiatry (Cambridge), and have held positions at Harvard Medical School, the University of Oxford, and the University of Cambridge.

Research

AI Ethics & Governance

The ethical and legal implications of artificial intelligence in healthcare and research, including the EU AI Act, generative AI in academic writing, and questions of credit and responsibility for AI-generated work.

Bioethics

The philosophical and legal foundations of medical ethics, including informed consent, human enhancement technologies, and the governance of patient-facing AI systems.

The Right to Science

The human right to enjoy the benefits of scientific progress under international law (Article 15, ICESCR). How this right bears on access to medicines, research participation, and science policy.

Science Acceleration

Frameworks for accelerating scientific and medical progress within ethical and legal boundaries, examining how regulatory and institutional structures can better support research and translation.
